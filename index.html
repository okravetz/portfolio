Bio

Oren is an Associate Design Director with formal training and experience in user research, product design, wireframing, interaction design, design systems, information architecture, usability testing, project management, and prototyping.

Oren's recent projects include creating a new tool for salespeople at American Express to use to prove their products' value to their clients, designing the new OliveGarden.com, and developing landing page templates for various American Express properties around the world. Prior to that, he worked on designing the new AllianceBernstein.com, designing the new BakerHughes.com, and helping Cisco update their Small Business landing pages.

One of that projects about which he is most passionate was a two-year detailed design effort for web-based patient and clinical portals for fertility clinics that have partnered with IntegraMed. He also worked closely with the developers to create a form generator tool for the client to be able to more easily manage the business rules for complex forms.

As an Associate Design Director at Isobar, Oren leads his teams through the design process from user research and product definition through launch and post-launch analytics. He was also instrumental in the design of the Isobar Design Hub, helping project teams more efficiently share work with clients. Between projects, Oren is also an active participant in various business resource groups.

Oren's experience at Isobar includes designing mobile apps, websites, and large-scale web applications for various clients including: Bloomberg, Sotheby's, MSCI, D. E. Shaw, NBCUniversal, Google, Umpqua Bank, The Wall Street Journal, IntegraMed, the U.S. Air Force, General Motors, Flexport, Alatar'd State, Baker Hughes, Cisco, Alliance Bernstein, Olive Garden, and American Express.

Oren's other interests include photography, typography, print design, mixology, home-brewing, reading, watching movies, TV, and online shows, playing video games and experimenting with the latest, cutting-edge technologies.

In 2008, Oren received his B.S. in Software Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis and in 2010, he received his Masters in Human-Computer Interaction from Carnegie Mellon University.
